> Projects

Supervised Food Recognition Models

Trained and compared the efficacy of several machine learning models in recognizing and grouping images of food. Of the three models we trained and tested, Convolutional Neural Networks consistently outperformed the K-Nearest Neighbors and Support Vector Machines algorithms as it offered a simplified algorithm with a low number of trainable parameters. While the SVM models managed to come close in terms of accuracy, the long time to train made it inefficient and costly.

User Attention State Versus Gaming Ability

Tested the correlation between a user's attention state and their gaming ability using Neurosky EEGs. We hypothesized that attention measure scores would be higher for inexperienced players and lower for experienced players as more experienced played may be less attentive as they are more familiar with the game. Our findings supported this hypothesis as well as showed periods of decreased attentiveness in both groups of players when clearing obstacles within the game, lowering stress levels.

Predicting Political Affiliation of US H.O.R. Members Through Stock Trades

Examined the relationship between the political affiliation of US House of Representaive Memebers and the stocks they traded. Trained on the datasets of representatives and transactions of 2020-2021, our final Random Forest Classifier model achieved a 92% accuracy when classifying female representatives and a 87% accuracy when classifying male representatives. We found it likely that, due to the larger number of male representatives as well as the number of female Democrats compared to female Republicans, the model most likely gave more weight to female datapoints.
